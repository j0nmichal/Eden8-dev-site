Closing the gap before

The STEAM equity gap isn't a middle school problem or a high school problem. It's a kindergarten problem. Eden 8 works where the window is still open.

Why This, Why Now

Aviation and aerospace are the fastest-growing workforce sectors. And almost no one is preparing students for them early enough.

The United States faces a critical shortage of trained pilots, aerospace engineers, drone operators, and aviation technicians. By 2032, industry estimates project a deficit of more than 200,000 aviation workers in North America alone.

The pipeline problem starts at the very beginning. Most K–5 students in Title 1 schools never encounter aviation, aerospace, or advanced manufacturing as a concept — let alone as a career possibility. By the time those opportunities surface in middle or high school, students have already formed fixed ideas about who this work is for.

We build the intervention that happens before that window closes — at the ages when curiosity is highest and identity is still forming.

Why Advanced Air Mobility

The sector where workforce need meets educational opportunity.

Advanced Air Mobility — drones, electric vertical takeoff aircraft, autonomous systems — is one of the most accessible entry points into aerospace for young learners. It's visible, tactile, and happening now.

Fastest-Growing Sector

The FAA projects a need for 17,000 new pilots annually through 2036. The drone industry alone is expected to generate 100,000+ jobs by 2030.

Early Exposure Changes Outcomes

Research consistently shows that early STEAM identity formation — before age 10 — is the single strongest predictor of career pursuit in technical fields. Title 1 students are the least likely to receive this exposure.

Standards-Aligned by Design

Mighty Tails curriculum maps directly to Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS) and Common Core, giving school districts a clear adoption path without additional burden on teachers.

Flagship Program

Mighty Tails

An original animated series built for kids, designed for classrooms, and optimized for the students who have the most to gain from early STEAM access.

Each episode of Mighty Tails follows characters navigating challenges rooted in real aviation and aerospace science. The storytelling is kid-forward and action-oriented. The science underneath it is rigorous and teacher-verified.

Paired with a standards-aligned K–5 curriculum, Mighty Tails gives educators a complete teaching system — not just a video to play. Lesson plans, activities, and vocabulary guides ship with every episode release.

What's Next

Mighty Tails is the first program. Not the last.

Eden 8 is a program-building institution. Mighty Tails demonstrates the model — high-quality, standards-aligned, animation-forward educational content deployed specifically in underserved communities.

Future programs will follow the same framework: identify a sector where workforce need is acute and early exposure is absent, build curriculum and content that meets students at their level, and deploy where the impact is highest.

We are not building a single show. We are building a methodology.
